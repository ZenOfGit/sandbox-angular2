Here we have a list of 4 hockey players initialized in our constructor.
When we click the Refresh Players button, we call a method that gets an object with the same name and content,
but it has one more player. We are looping though these players with the structural directive *ngFor, (which
needs the * because structural directives change the DOM) and in a large list, *ngFor may perform poorly. When
Whemn we click to get the players, Angular tears the td and tr elements down and recretes them. We can use
trackBy with the *ngFor directive to track by an id rather than object references, and Angular will keep track
of our hockey players and only replace the new one(s) which improves performance on large lists.
We have also added a few properties of *ngFor like i, isFirst, isLast, isOdd, and isEven to display them.
